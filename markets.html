Mexican markets

Mexican Markets are very important to us as a restaurant. Firstly because they are our food providers but also because they are part of the Mexican culture.We need as society not let them disappeared by the big multinational companies, otherwise we'd lost a big part of our culture.

That is why we maintain a close relationship with most of the local markets. We try to buy to all of them, it is important to mention that after a research we found more profitable to make business with the local markets.

Mercado Jauregui

It is a traditional market with a main focus of sea food. Mexico is famed for its gastronomy. But beyond the tacos and guacamole of world renown, there are rich and various culinary traditions spanning the whole country. The tastes, smells and textures of traditional Mexican ingredients are an assault on the senses, especially the more exotic items such as maguey worms, ants and fried grasshoppers.

  • Cultural activities: pottery, artisans.
  • Address: Maximiliano 123. Xalapa, Mexico
  • Hours: Monday to Wednesday, 11:00am to 11:00pm

Mercado Galeana

It is famous by its wooden toys but also for its great variety of vegetables and fruits. Contemporary chefs are continually developing Mexican culinary identity, incorporating ingredients and cooking styles from all over the world in a cosmopolitan fusion. For the Aztecs, there was no difference between artists and artisans, so pottery, basket making, and weaving were all part of the ancient culture's artistic production. All these things you can find them in the Xalapa Mexican Markets.

  • Cultural activities: pottery, artisans.
  • Address: Revolucion 133. Xalapa, Mexico.
  • Hours: Monday to Wednesday, 11:00am to 11:00pm

Mercado Xalapa

TIt is the biggest market in the city, it has all kind of food, clothes, all you can imagine. The most impressive thing is the tortillas produced there. Nowadays, skilled Mexican hands keep ancient traditions alive in countless villages. Complex patterned rugs from looms in Teotitlan, Oaxaca, unique pottery pieces from Quiroga, Michoacan, and the world-renowned Talavera ceramics from Puebla, are just a sample of the wide variety of artisan crafts Mexico offers for the culturally curious. All these things you can make them in the Xalapa Mexican Markets!!!

  • Cultural activities: pottery, artisans.
  • Address: Constitucion 425. Xalapa, Mexico.
  • Hours: Monday to Wednesday, 11:00am to 11:00pm
